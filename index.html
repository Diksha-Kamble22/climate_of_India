Climate of India

The climate of India consists of a wide range of weather conditions across a vast geographic scale and varied topography, making generalizations difficult. Also, based on the Köppen system, India hosts six major climatic sub ging from rid deserts in the west, alpine tundra and glaciers in the north, and humid tropical regions supporting rain forests in the southwest and the island territoriate.

•The country's meteorological department follows the international standard of four seasons with some local adjustments:
